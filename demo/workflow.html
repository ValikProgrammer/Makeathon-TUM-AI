lease-a-kitchen — Agentic Outbound Workflow

Multi-agent prospecting pipeline · GDPR + UWG §7 compliant by design
✓ Inverted contact direction avoids cold-call restrictions

AGENTS

Jack
Research agent — finds prospects worth contacting
Kate
Orchestration + voice agent — triggers outreach, qualifies, follows up
Otto
Offer agent — generates tailored proposals
STEP 1
Jack

Prospect Discovery

Scans public sources for high-intent B2B signals — only leads worth contacting pass the filter.

  • Commercial registry
  • Trade press & openings
  • Job boards & funding news
  • Per-lead signal documented
GDPR Art. 6(1)(f) · legitimate interest, B2B public sources
▶
STEP 2
Kate

Postcard → QR → Call-me

Personalized postcard ships with QR code. Prospect scans → landing page → consents → requests call-back.

  • Postcard with Art. 14 notice
  • Landing page with AI-consent checkbox
  • Inbound call to Kate (voice AI)
  • Qualification in <3 min
UWG §7 N/A · postal + inbound = no marketing restriction
▶
STEP 3
Otto

Offer Generation

Based on qualification data, Otto matches available kitchens and drafts a tailored proposal.

  • Kitchen inventory match
  • Pricing by dates & scope
  • Terms & availability
  • PDF + email-ready format
Internal processing · no external comms, data-minimized
▶
STEP 4
Kate

Delivery & Follow-up

Kate sends the offer via email and follows up by phone — within the consent already given.

  • Email with offer + opt-out
  • Up to 2 follow-up calls max
  • AI disclosure in every call
  • Instant suppression on objection
Pre-contractual comms · outside UWG §7 advertising scope

✓ LOAD-BEARING COMPLIANCE CONTROLS

CONSENT
Landing page checkbox explicitly covers AI-conducted calls. Logged with IP, timestamp, version.
DISCLOSURE
Kate self-identifies as AI within the first seconds of every call (AI Act Art. 50).
INFO DUTY
Article 14 GDPR notice printed on every postcard — source, controller, purpose, objection.
FREQUENCY
Hard cap: 2 follow-up calls per offer. Immediate suppression on verbal objection.
